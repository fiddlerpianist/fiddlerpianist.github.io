Things to keep in mind about the camp:

What to bring:

•      All bedding and pillows (night-time lows will be in the 30s or low 40s)

•      Towel and toiletries

•      Sunscreen (very important)

•      Mosquito repellant ( West Nile virus was very bad here last year)

•      Water bottle (extremely important—it’s very easy to dehydrate and feel sick)

•      Clothes to handle very changeable weather conditions. We may have any or all of the following: hot (up to 80s) and sunny, cold with rain or even snow, thunderstorms, hail, and windy conditions. The weather is usually nice this time of year, but fronts can come in quickly and bring fierce conditions.

•      Morris kit (duh!)

 

About the altitude:

Most people do fine when they come to the Front Range , but expect to feel easily winded and to have reduced alcohol tolerance. A mild headache that responds to aspirin or ibuprofen is also common. Anything worse could be a sign of altitude sickness. If someone on your team is feeling really sick, come talk to one of the locals.

Your best defense is to drink lots of water, quite a bit more than you are used to drinking at home. It’s easy to get dehydrated, and even mild dehydration can make altitude adjustment, hangovers, and other things much worse.

Take a cue from the locals—no matter what goes wrong, blame it on the altitude!

 

Other Things:

The buildings are quite old and need to be treated with care. No smoking, except along the gravel driveway.

Water supply in the mountains is always chancey, and we are having a pretty bad drought right now. Try to keep your showers quick!

There is really no security at the camp and it will be mostly unattended during the days when we are away. Think twice before leaving money and valuables behind.
